\begin{abstract}
We investigate imitation learning for a robotic pick-and-place task in which a SO-101 arm picks oranges and places them on a plate. Training data are collected inside NVIDIA Isaac Sim, edited manually with a custom GUI, and models are trained on the EPFL SCITAS cluster using the LeRobot framework. We compare two policy architectures---ACT (Action Chunking with Transformers) and SmolVLA (a compact Vision-Language-Action model)---and evaluate their success rates under various dataset sizes and training regimes.
