# Report Writing Guidelines

## Length
Max 20 pages — quality over quantity.

Presentation with thesis writing advice: https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1wdk6iVC3G0WgORenrWnNZ3Llxan1iO6rcQS7SlbRQxU/edit?usp=sharing

## Structure

### Introduction
- What is the problem
- What is the goal (include review or related state-of-the-art to show what has already been explored and hence what is the goal of this work)
- What is the hypothesis you are proposing/testing
- What do you show or demonstrate in your work
- What are the key contributions

**Optional sub-section: Problem Statement**

### Methods
- What methods have you developed, or what experimental processes have you undertaken? This could include model formation, simulation formulation, and fabrication methods.
- This section could include figures that relate to the methods

### Results
- Experimental results
  - Try and avoid time series data unless absolutely necessary
  - The experiments should map back to your hypothesis in the introduction

### Discussion & Conclusion
- What has been learnt/contributed and how do these answer the hypothesis
- What improvements/further extensions could be made

### References

### Supplementary Information

## General Advice
- A good report should not read like a list of things you have done, but a structured reflection on them
- You want to show you can think and represent your work in a scientific manner. This means creating a hypothesis which you then test and evaluate.
- Sometimes knowing what to leave out is as important as what to put in
- Make sure figures have units and labels. Every figure should have a message.
